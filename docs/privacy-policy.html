Splits is a desktop application for timing interval training sessions and distributing performance reports to athletes. This policy explains what data the application handles and how it is used.

Data You Provide

Splits does not collect, transmit, or store any data on external servers. All data you work with in the application — including athlete rosters and session results — is created and managed by you and stored exclusively on your local device. This data is never sent anywhere except when you explicitly choose to email a report (see below).

Google Sign-In and Gmail

Splits includes an optional feature that lets you send performance reports to athletes via Gmail. To use this feature, you sign in with your Google account using OAuth 2.0. Specifically:

  • The sign-in flow opens your browser to Google's consent screen. You grant the application permission to send email on your behalf.
  • Google issues an OAuth refresh token, which is stored locally on your device (in your user data directory) so you do not need to sign in again each session.
  • This token is never transmitted to any server controlled by Splits or its developers.
  • Emails are sent only when you explicitly initiate a send action within the app. The app will not send email autonomously.
  • The content of emails (HTML report and recipient addresses) passes through the Gmail API directly from your device to Google's servers and then to the recipients you specify.

Splits requests only the minimum Gmail scope required to send email (gmail.send). It does not read, modify, or access any of your existing email.

Revoking Access

You can sign out of Google at any time from within the Splits settings panel. You can also revoke the app's access to your Google account at any time by visiting myaccount.google.com/permissions.

No Analytics or Tracking

Splits does not include any analytics, crash reporting, telemetry, or tracking of any kind. The application has no network communication other than the Gmail API calls you explicitly initiate.

Children's Privacy

Splits is intended for use by coaches and instructors. The application does not knowingly collect any personal information from children.
